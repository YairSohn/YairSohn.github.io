Pawns

Pawns can move and attack in four different ways

    1. Moving forward 1 square
    2. Moving forward 2 squares (only when moving the pawn for the first time)
    3. Attacking another piece diagonally
    4. En Passant (an advanced move that will be covered later)
Note: Pawns Cannot move backwards. Also, if you manage to get a pawn to the other side of the board, it can be promoted to any piece (except for a king)

Knights

    Knights move in a L shape. They move two squares then one as follows
    Knights are the only pieces which can jump over other pieces

Bishops

    Bishops can only move diagnoally. Therefore, a dark square bishop can only stay on dark squares, and a light squared bishop can only stay on light squares
    bishops have unlimited range.

Queens

Queens can move in any direction with unlimited range.

Rooks

    Rooks can only move in s straight line. Therefore, a dark square bishop can only stay on dark squares, and a light squared bishop can only stay on light squares
    bishops have unlimited range.

Kings

    Kings can move 1 square in any direction
    A king is not allowed to move itself into check (under attack). To do so would be an illegal move
